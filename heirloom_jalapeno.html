Capsicum annuum

Heirloom Jalapeño

A classic jalapeño with dependable flavor, medium heat, and all-purpose kitchen use.

Overview

Perhaps the most well-known pepper in the world, the jalapeño packs a slight punch without being overwhelming. This heirloom variety is versatile, used in fresh salsa, soups, roasted dishes, and even spicy margaritas. Commonly picked when green, the jalapeño will ripen to a deep red if left on the plant.

Notes

The heirloom jalapeño is one of the most recognizable peppers in the world and remains a staple in home gardens, grocery stores, and kitchens everywhere. It offers a clean, grassy flavor with a manageable level of heat, which makes it one of the most versatile peppers to grow and use.

These peppers are commonly harvested while green, but if left on the plant they will ripen to red and develop a slightly sweeter, fuller flavor. Whether used fresh, pickled, roasted, or blended into salsa, the jalapeño remains one of the most popular peppers in the world for good reason.
