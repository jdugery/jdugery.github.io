Pepper Varieties

A growing catalog of peppers at Red Hot Philly Peppers, organized by species.

Capsicum annuum

Classic garden peppers ranging from sweet to hot.

Heirloom Jalapeño

Capsicum annuum

Traditional jalapeño with dependable production, thick walls, and classic medium heat.

Jalapeño LMT (RHPP Patented)

Capsicum annuum

A signature RHPP variety - this jalapeño has been engineered for supermarket level size.

Poblano

Capsicum annuum

Mild to spicy, depending on the pod. This rich-flavored pepper is commonly known as Ancho when dried.

Serrano

Capsicum annuum

Bright, crisp, and hotter than a jalapeño with excellent fresh flavor.

Cayenne

Capsicum annuum

Long, thin pepper known for sharp heat and excellent drying qualities. Popular as a powder.

California Wonder Sweet

Capsicum annuum

Classic sweet bell pepper with thick walls, crisp texture, and reliable yields.

Red Marconi

Capsicum annuum

Long Italian sweet pepper that is especially good for roasting and fresh eating.

Capsicum chinense

Aromatic, fruity peppers from low-heat rarities to brutal superhots.

Trinidad Perfume

Capsicum chinense

Highly aromatic chinense with habanero-like flavor and almost no heat.

Naga Bubblegum

Capsicum chinense

Superhot pepper known for extreme heat and distinctive color bleeding into the stem.

Pimenta da Dog

Capsicum chinense

Rare chinense type with distinctive pod shape and strong heat.

Hallow's Eve

Capsicum chinense

Dark ornamental superhot with dramatic appearance and serious heat. As the name implies, this is a spooky pepper.

Red Habanero

Capsicum chinense

Classic habanero with fruity flavor, strong heat, and heavy production.

Orange Habanero

Capsicum chinense

Bright citrusy flavor with the classic habanero burn.

Ghost

Capsicum chinense

Also known as Bhut Jolokia, this pepper originating from northern India was once crowned the hottest in the world.

Peach Ghost

Capsicum chinense

Peach-colored ghost type with floral notes and strong superhot intensity.

Trinidad Moruga Scorpion

Capsicum chinense

A Carribbean landrace variety from the village of Moruga on the island of Trinidad, this heavy-hitting superhot packs brutal heat and fruity undertones.

Armageddon

Capsicum chinense

As the name implies, an extremely hot modern superhot bred out of the UK for aggressive heat and bold flavor. This was the first superhot grown at the RHPP farm.

Capsicum baccatum

Distinctive, often fruity peppers with unique shapes and excellent flavor.

Brazilian Starfish

Capsicum baccatum

Beautiful winged pepper resembling a starfish. Fruity flavor and mild to medium heat.

Sugar Rush Stripey Peach

Capsicum baccatum

This sweet, fruity baccatum almost looks like something out of a candy store.

Capsicum frutescens

Small, productive peppers known for sharp heat and sauce-friendly pods.

Thai Chili

Capsicum frutescens

Small, highly productive pepper with fast sharp heat and versatile kitchen use.

Tabasco

Capsicum frutescens

Classic juicy upright pepper famous for it's namesake sauce and bright, vinegar-friendly heat.
